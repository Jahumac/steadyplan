About

Built to face the numbers

A self-hosted planning tool built from real-life mistakes, late learning, and the need to see the whole picture clearly.

SteadyPlan started as a personal project, but not from a place of perfect discipline or polished financial knowledge.

For a long time, money was something I reacted to rather than understood. I had been through debt, an IVA, and years of making decisions without a proper system. Some of it came from not knowing better. Some of it came from old habits, pressure, and simply not facing the full picture clearly enough.

The turning point was realising that avoiding the numbers does not make them smaller. It only makes life foggier.

I needed one place where I could see everything properly: accounts, debts, savings, investments, goals, pension contributions, and long-term plans. Not spread across apps, notes, spreadsheets and guesswork. Not locked inside another cloud platform asking for all my data. Just a calm, honest view of reality.

That is what SteadyPlan became.

Why I built it

I did not build SteadyPlan as a finance guru or someone pretending to have all the answers.

I built it as someone who learned later than he wishes he had.

I grew up without strong financial guidance, made avoidable mistakes, and only started taking investing and long-term planning seriously much later in life. Once that clicked, I realised how powerful clarity can be. Not hype. Not “get rich quick”. Just understanding where you are, what matters, and what small consistent decisions can do over time.

SteadyPlan is the tool I wanted when I was still trying to make sense of all that.

Who it is for

SteadyPlan is for people who want to:

  • face their own numbers without flinching
  • keep their data under their own control
  • think longer-term
  • make calmer decisions
  • and build financial steadiness without needing to become day-trading weirdos or spreadsheet fanatics

It is especially for ordinary people who feel like they came to this late.

If investing once felt abstract or intimidating, or if you are simply trying to get more intentional with money, I know that feeling.

What it is

SteadyPlan is a self-hosted finance planning and visibility tool.

  • It runs on your own server, NAS or desktop.
  • It stores your data locally in SQLite.
  • It is designed to be practical, direct, and understandable.
  • It helps connect day-to-day choices with the bigger picture.

What it is not

It is not a bank, a broker, budgeting guru or financial adviser. It does not promise outcomes. Projections are estimates based on your inputs.

Why share it

I use SteadyPlan myself.

It helps me stay honest about progress, retirement planning, contributions, and whether the life I say I want is actually supported by the numbers underneath it.

I am also someone who values self-hosting, open-source software, repairability, and owning your own tools. I have benefited from other people building useful things and sharing them openly. SteadyPlan is my attempt to contribute something back.

This is a one-person project, built with AI-assisted development, testing, and a lot of iteration. It is not trying to become another generic cloud finance app.

It exists because facing the numbers matters.

And if it helps someone else stop avoiding their financial reality and start building a calmer one, then it is doing exactly what I hoped it would do.
