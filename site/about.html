About

Built to face the numbers

SteadyPlan started as a personal project.

A few years ago, my financial life looked very different. I had been through serious debt, an IVA, and the kind of money stress where looking too closely at the numbers felt easier to avoid than to face.

The turning point was not a magic trick. It was visibility.

I needed to see the whole picture clearly: accounts, debts, savings, investments, goals, contributions and retirement plans. Not scattered across banking apps, spreadsheets and mental arithmetic. Not hidden behind another service asking for all my data. Just one calm place where I could understand where I was and what needed attention.

That is what SteadyPlan became.

What it is

SteadyPlan is a self-hosted planning and visibility tool for people who want to face their own numbers, keep their data under their control, and make steadier long-term decisions.

  • It runs on your own server/NAS/desktop (Docker/Unraid-friendly).
  • It stores your data locally in a SQLite database.
  • You can use it from desktop, tablet, or phone browser on your LAN/VPN. Where supported, it can be installed as a web app (PWA).

What it is not

It is not a bank, broker, budgeting guru or financial adviser. It does not promise outcomes. Projections are estimates based on your inputs.

Why share it

I use SteadyPlan myself to stay on track — to understand progress, check whether retirement plans still make sense, and keep day-to-day choices connected to the bigger picture.

It is also shaped by the self-hosting and open-source tools I value: systems you can inspect, repair, back up and own. I have benefited from open-source projects for years; sharing SteadyPlan is one small way to give something back.

This is a one-person project, built with AI-assisted development, testing, and a lot of careful iteration. It is not trying to become another cloud finance platform.

It exists because facing the numbers matters.

If SteadyPlan helps someone else stop avoiding the picture and start making calmer, clearer decisions, then it is doing what I built it to do.
