About SteadyPlan

Built to face the numbers

A self-hosted planning tool built from real-life mistakes, late learning, and the need to see the whole picture clearly.

Before

Money was something to react to, not understand

SteadyPlan did not come from perfect discipline or polished financial confidence. It came from debt, an IVA, stress, avoidance, and years of not having a proper system for seeing the full picture clearly.

Some of that came from not knowing better. Some of it came from old habits, pressure, and simply not facing the numbers honestly enough.

Turning point

Visibility mattered more than any clever trick

Avoiding the numbers does not make them smaller. It only makes life foggier.

The real need was one place to understand accounts, debts, savings, investments, goals, pension contributions and long-term plans without scattering everything across apps, notes, spreadsheets and guesswork.

Why I built it

I built the tool I wanted earlier

I did not build SteadyPlan as a finance guru or someone pretending to have all the answers.

I built it as someone who learned later than he wishes he had — someone who only started taking investing and long-term planning seriously after enough mistakes to know what confusion costs.

Not hype. Not get-rich-quick thinking. Just understanding where you are, what matters, and what small consistent decisions can do over time.

Who it is for

Ordinary people who want clarity more than hype

  • People who feel they came to this later than they wish they had
  • People who want calmer decisions instead of financial theatre
  • People who want to keep their data under their own control
  • People who want a clearer picture without becoming spreadsheet fanatics

If investing or planning once felt abstract, intimidating, or easy to avoid, SteadyPlan is built with that feeling in mind.

What it is

A practical self-hosted planning and visibility tool

  • Runs on your own server, NAS, or desktop
  • Stores your data locally in SQLite
  • Helps connect monthly choices with the bigger picture
  • Uses honest wording around assumptions, estimates, and trade-offs

What it is not

Not a bank, broker, guru, or promise engine

SteadyPlan is not a bank, a broker, a social finance platform, or a financial adviser.

It does not promise outcomes. Scenario estimates are illustrative and based on your inputs, assumptions, and scenarios.

It is also not trying to become another generic cloud finance app dressed up as certainty.

Why share it

This is a contribution, not a performance

I use SteadyPlan myself. It helps me stay honest about progress, contributions, retirement planning, and whether the life I say I want is actually supported by the numbers underneath it.

I also care about self-hosting, open-source software, repairability, and owning your own tools. I have benefited from other people building useful things and sharing them openly. SteadyPlan is my attempt to contribute something back.

It is a one-person project, built with AI-assisted development, testing, and careful iteration. The human reason comes first.

It exists because facing the numbers matters.

If it helps someone else stop avoiding their financial reality and start building a calmer one, then it is doing exactly what I hoped it would do.
