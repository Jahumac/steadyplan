Overview

Start with the answer

The Overview exists to reduce avoidance. It should tell you where you stand, what matters now, and where the pressure points are before you dig into configuration or detail.

This is especially important for the roadmap: answer-first hierarchy is part of making SteadyPlan more inviting and more useful to normal people, not just finance power users.

Accessible vs locked money

Useful distinctions, not just bigger totals

One of SteadyPlan’s most important jobs is helping users understand what they can actually use now, what comes with restrictions or penalties, and what is really part of later retirement planning.

That distinction supports calmer decisions. It stops the product from presenting one big number as if all money is equally available.

This principle runs through Overview, Planning, and future assistant/API work as well.

Why it matters

  • Shows what is realistically available now
  • Makes pensions and longer-term holdings easier to reason about
  • Supports calmer affordability and planning decisions

Holdings and accounts

Keep investments and account context in the same picture

SteadyPlan helps track holdings without requiring bank or broker account linking. You can keep prices fully manual or enable optional external lookups where that suits you.

The goal is not market excitement. It is making the investment part of your life legible alongside everything else.

Projections

Scenario planning without pretending the future is guaranteed

Projections let you explore what changes if you adjust contributions, retirement timing, or assumptions. They are there to support thinking, not to manufacture certainty.

That honest boundary matters. The product should help people think longer-term while staying clear that estimates are still estimates.

Trust and ownership

Self-hosting only feels safe if the boundaries are clear

SteadyPlan is not just about keeping data local in theory. It also needs to make backups, exports, restore behaviour, and optional external lookups understandable in practice.

That is why Settings, privacy notes, and backup guidance are part of the public story as well as the app itself.

Trust surfaces include per-user JSON exports, whole-instance SQLite backups, and plain-language notes on public exposure and optional external lookups.
