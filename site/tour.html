Product tour

SteadyPlan runs on your own server/NAS/desktop, and you use it in a browser (desktop, tablet, or phone). This tour explains the main areas and why they exist. Screenshots use demo data.

Overview

What it does: gives you one place to see the current picture across accounts, goals and the month-to-month rhythm.

Why it exists: when the numbers are visible, it’s easier to stay honest and make small course corrections.

Demo data · Open screenshot

Contributions / monthly update

What it does: helps you keep your month-to-month inputs consistent so projections stay grounded in reality.

Why it exists: long-term plans drift when the monthly picture isn’t reviewed. A light routine keeps the model trustworthy.

No screenshot on the public site for this yet.

Holdings

What it does: track holdings without bank/broker linking. Prices can be fully manual, or you can enable optional external lookups.

Why it exists: it’s easier to plan when your investments aren’t split across apps and mental arithmetic.

Projections

What it does: scenario estimates for retirement planning, driven by your current balances, contribution settings and assumptions.

Why it exists: you can see what changes if you contribute more, retire earlier/later, or adjust assumptions — without pretending it’s a promise.

Demo data · Open screenshot

Settings and data ownership

What it does: keeps the “trust boundaries” clear — where your data lives, what you can export, and what assumptions feed projections.

Why it exists: self-hosting is only comforting if you understand how backups, exports and restores actually work.

Demo data · Open screenshot

Backups / restore confidence

What it does: gives you two layers: per-user JSON exports and whole-instance SQLite backups, plus guidance for server-level recovery.

Why it exists: planning is only useful if you trust that your data can be recovered after mistakes or updates.
