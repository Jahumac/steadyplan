Why SteadyPlan exists

Built to face the numbers

SteadyPlan started as a personal project after serious money stress, debt, and the slow realisation that avoiding the full picture only makes life foggier.

The turning point was not a trick. It was visibility. One place to understand accounts, debts, savings, investments, goals, contributions and retirement plans honestly.

That is still the heart of the product now: helping ordinary people face the numbers more calmly and more intentionally.

What it is and is not

A planning tool, not a promise engine

What it is

  • A self-hosted personal finance planning and visibility tool
  • A bridge between monthly life and long-term goals
  • A practical product that values local control and honest wording

What it is not

  • Not a bank, broker, or budgeting guru persona
  • Not a financial adviser
  • Not a race to generic hosted SaaS

Safest default is LAN or VPN access. Public exposure is optional and should be set up deliberately with HTTPS and a trusted reverse proxy.

Trust, ownership, and first impression

Open about the product. Clear about the boundaries.

A better website should not just look nicer. It should make the product easier to understand and easier to trust.

Privacy and data ownership

Local SQLite storage, no cloud sync, and plain-language notes on what leaves your machine when optional lookups are enabled.

Backups and restore confidence

See how per-user JSON exports, whole-instance SQLite backups, restore preview checks, and automatic pre-restore safety backups fit together before you trust the app with real data.

Use it from desktop, tablet, or phone

SteadyPlan runs on your own instance. Use it in a browser, and install it as a web app where PWA support is available.
